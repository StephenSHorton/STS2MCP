# STS2 Hero & Playstyle Guide

## The Regent — Star Mechanic

### Resource: Stars
- Stars are a secondary resource. Many powerful cards require stars to play.
- **Divine Right** (starting relic) gives 3 stars at combat start. Budget carefully.
- Star generators: **Venerate+** (1E → 3 stars), **Gather Light** (1E → 7 block + 1 star), **Solar Strike** (1E → 8 dmg + 1 star).

### Key Cards
- **Falling Star+** — MVP. 0 energy + 2 stars, 11 damage + Weak + Vulnerable. Play after skills to maximize Slow stacking.
- **Crescent Spear** — 1E + 1 star, 15 base + 2 per star-cost card in deck. Count your star-cost cards — can easily reach 20+ damage.
- **Reflect** — 1E + 3 stars, 17 Block + reflects blocked damage back. Game-changing vs. multi-attacker fights.
- **Monologue** — 0 cost, +1 Strength per card played this turn. Play FIRST, then chain as many cards as possible.
- **Gather Light** — Strictly better Defend (7 block + 1 star vs 5 block). Always take over base Defend.
- **Radiate** — 0 cost, deals 3 × (stars gained this turn) to ALL enemies. Play AFTER generating stars.
- **Shining Strike** — 1E, 6–8 dmg + 2 stars + returns to draw pile. Excellent star engine; plays itself again next cycle.
- **Tyranny** — Power: start of turn draw 1, exhaust 1. Strong vs. status-card spam (Toxic etc.) — free exhaust clears junk without using energy.
- **Quasar+** — 0E + 2 stars. Adds an upgraded colorless card to hand (not deck) — flexible power without deck bloat. Boosts Crescent Spear count.
- **Particle Wall** — 0E + 2 stars, 9 block, returns to hand. Can be played repeatedly each turn as long as you have stars. With Venerate+ and Gather Light generating stars, you can stack 30+ block in one turn defensively.

### Combat Sequencing (Regent-Specific)
1. **Monologue first** (if in hand) — every subsequent card gives +1 Strength.
2. **Star generators next** (Venerate, Gather Light, Solar Strike) — unlock star-cost cards.
3. **Radiate** — play after all star generation for maximum hits.
4. **Falling Star+** — apply Weak/Vulnerable before other big attacks.
5. **Crescent Spear / Guiding Star** — biggest hits last, benefit from full Slow/Strength stacking.

### Star Budget Planning
- Venerate+ (1E) gives 3 stars. Reflect costs 3 stars. Crescent Spear costs 1. Plan ahead.
- If you play Venerate+ and then Reflect, you have 0 stars left — can't play Crescent Spear that turn.
- Gather Light + Solar Strike together give 2 stars, enough for Falling Star+.
- **Stars carry over between turns** within a combat. Unspent stars persist to next turn.
- **Radiate counts stars gained this turn**, not total stars. Generate stars THEN play Radiate. Divine Right's 3 combat-start stars count as "gained this turn" for Radiate — so Turn 1 Radiate always hits at least 3 times.
- **Per-combat powers** (Child of Stars, Tyranny, Monologue Strength) do NOT persist between combats. Re-play them each fight.
- **Tyranny exhaust: exhaust highest star-cost cards you can't afford.** When you have 0-1 stars, Reflect (3★), Astral Pulse (3★), Quasar+ (2★), and Particle Wall (2★) are dead weight — exhaust them first. Keep star generators and playable attacks.
- **Critically low HP means block first, stars second.** Below ~15 HP with no Gather Light/Particle Wall in hand, you cannot generate stars AND build sufficient block in one turn. Sometimes the correct play is 2× Defend+ and no offense.

### Slow Debuff (Enemy Power)
- Some enemies (e.g. Bygone Effigy) have Slow: each card you play makes them take 10% more Attack damage that turn.
- Against Slow enemies, play as many non-attack cards before attacks as possible.
- Multi-hit attacks (Radiate) benefit enormously from high Slow stacks.

### Elite: Decimillipede (Act 2)
- Three segments (Front/Middle/Back) each with **Reattach**: if ANY other segment is alive when a segment dies, it revives in 2 turns with 25 HP.
- **Cannot permanently kill a segment while others live** — Reattach always triggers. Plan to kill the last survivor alone.
- Winning strategy: whittle all segments down, then kill two simultaneously (AoE), leaving one alone → kill it before the others revive.
- Alternatively: use **Reflect** to let segments attack into your block and kill themselves. Segments that die this way still Reattach if others survive, but Reflect can weaken them all quickly.
- After clearing two segments, you have 2 turns to kill the third before the first revived segment returns. Prioritize burst.
- Segments grow Strength each turn — don't drag the fight out.

### Elite: Infested Prism (Act 2)
- **Vital Spark**: First time it takes attack damage each turn, you gain +1 energy. Always include at least one attack each turn to bank this refund — it effectively gives you a 4-energy turn.
- **Attack pattern escalates**: Turn 1 = Attack 16, Turn 2 = Attack 16 + Defend (gains ~16 block), Turn 3+ = Attack 9×3 = 27 total. Plan block requirements accordingly — 27 incoming requires 26+ block to survive at 1 HP.
- **Apply Weak before each attack**: Falling Star+ reduces its attack by 25% (27→~20, 16→12). Against the 9×3 pattern, Weak alone is not enough to survive at low HP — you also need substantial block.
- **Particle Wall loop is the ideal defense**: Use Venerate+ (1E, +3★) → Particle Wall ×3 (0E+2★ each, returns each time) → 27 block in one turn. Stack more with Gather Light.
- **It rebuilds block every other turn** via Defend. Break through its block before dealing HP damage, or accept that some turns are wasted on chip damage.

### Boss: Kin Priest (Act 1)
- 190 HP boss with two Minion followers (~55-60 HP each).
- **Ignore followers** — they flee when the Priest dies.
- Followers alternate Buff (+2 Strength) and Attack. Fight gets harder every turn.
- Priest alternates Attack+Debuff (Frail/Weak) and Buff (+2 Strength).
- Reflect is incredibly strong — multiple enemies attacking into your block = massive reflected damage.
- Use Flex Potion + Monologue together for burst damage turns.
